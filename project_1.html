Project 1

Make the campus more friendly for visiting students from the animal kingdom

By Lam Yeung Kong Sunny

Why we need such Project?

Overview

Needfinding

  • User Characteristics: Identify traits of animal students, like form, abilities, language and habits.
  • Needs and Obstacles: Analyze challenges that are not user friendly for these students that might face on campus through community engagement.

Ideation

  • Brainstorm issues with existing campus facilities and services that may affect animal students.
  • Combine insight into a POV using mindmap.

Goal

Identify and address the unique challenges of animal students at HKUST to promote inclusivity and support.

Our Needfinding Process

One of the Interview

Interview is an essential step for our group to fully understand the needs from different kinds of people. In the interviews, we have interview people from different background, including countries or regions from Hong Kong, Mainland China, Turkey, Brazil and Iran, and different position, like undergraduate, phd student and teacher. The questions we have asked them are following:

  • Where are you from?
  • During your time on campus, did you encounter any problems or challenges that you consider unfriendly?
  • What is your favourite animal?
  • Assuming the animal is a student, like the second question, what problems or challenges you think it will encounter?

The interviews allow us to analyse POV in details in later section.

Field Visit

The food selling machine in the canteen at HKUST classifies food according to its cultural traits, which affords all students, including those from the animal kingdom, great diversity of choice. However, there is no option for users to exclude food that may be harmful due to allergies, which can be risky for some animal students.

Moreover, the design of the campus stair steps presents a problem for other species; smaller animals such as rabbits may find the steps too big while larger animals, like elephants, may find the steps too small. Improving these aspects would improve inclusivity and accessibility for all students on campus.

Our Ideation Process

Brainstorm

We have brainstormed a few POV based on our needfinding. Here are some examples.

Rabbit

  • User: A rabbit navigating campus stairways.
  • Need: Steps that are appropriately sized for smaller animals, hence they are easily access and increase safety.
  • Insight: The oversized steps are hard for them to use and will be dangerous.

Ant

  • User: An ant trying to travel across campus.
  • Need: Avoidance of trampling by bigger fauna and safe pathways for movement.
  • Insight: There may be frequenct accidents and seomtimes lethal if large animals do not notice small animals are near them.

Chicken

  • User: A chicken looking for food options.
  • Need: The ability to avoid food items that are inappropriate or highly allergic for chickens, if any.
  • Insight: Without a filtering option, the chicken may ingest negative food options which can result in a plethora of health issues as well as stress. Also, chicken won't eat chicken !!!

Mindmap formation

The mindmap is created with 3 categories, i.e. animal characteristics, need & challenges and solutions.

In the mindmap, we proposed different solutions with different problems. For instance, for the large stair step problem,we think that stairs with different step-height should be aligned next to each other. And for the small animals travel problem, we think that placing a sensor under the shoes of large animals allow instant awareness from the vibration of the sensor if small animals are near the shoes.

Presentation

In our presentation, we talked about the problem regarding to the food and chicken, we recommend putting in place a system where they can directly enter their dietary preferences on the school webpage. For instance, whenever a student animal touches their student ID card on the food selling machine, the machine captures their preset preferences and omits any undesired food selections. In the case where food item filtering is done, and no suitable options are left, the user interface will give way to a view of a delivery service located off the campus. It allows the students to get fast food deliveries tailored to the individual’s requirements. This not only improves the general animal student’s dining experience, but also their health and preferences.
